This is a safe zone and cropping tool with a few bonus checks
FrameCheck is primarily built for previewing safe zones, checking aspect ratio crops, and catching obvious structural defects like baked-in bars or edge slivers. The audio section is a lightweight bonus flag — it can catch clipping or flag an extremely quiet export, but it is not a substitute for proper loudness metering (your DAW's integrated LUFS meter is always more accurate). This tool does not replace human QC. It cannot read text, so it will never catch typos, misspellings, wrong copy, incorrect legal lines, or bad supers. It cannot judge timing, pacing, brand tone, or whether the edit actually makes sense. It cannot verify platform-specific safe zone compliance beyond the static overlay shown here. It does not check for dark starts/ends, black frames, freeze frames, or brightness issues — sampling cannot reliably distinguish those from intentional creative choices. Every video still requires a detailed human watch-through before approval — eyes on every frame, reading every word on screen. Use FrameCheck to speed up the mechanical checks, not to skip the human ones.
